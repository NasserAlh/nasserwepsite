Proposal to Amend the Audit Charter

1. Background and Issue Identification

At KPI, the integrity and effectiveness of our Internal Audit (IA) function are paramount. We have identified a critical governance issue where the Chief Executive Officer (CEO) currently holds the authority to alter internal auditors' appraisal and evaluation ratings. This practice has raised concerns regarding potential retaliatory actions against auditors, particularly in sensitive audit situations.

2. Risks Associated with Current Practice

Allowing the CEO to override auditor evaluations can lead to several significant risks, including:

  • Compromised Auditor Independence: Auditors might feel pressured to modify findings or soften reports because they fear adverse evaluations.
  • Reduced Objectivity: Concerns about potential retaliation can deter auditors from conducting thorough, unbiased audits.
  • Degradation of Audit Quality: The quality and reliability of audits may be compromised, undermining the company's risk management and governance frameworks.
  • Lowered Morale and Professionalism: The perception of a lack of support can affect morale and hinder the attraction and retention of qualified audit professionals.

3. Proposed Amendments to the Audit Charter

To mitigate these risks and reinforce the independence of the IA function, we propose the following amendments to the Audit Charter:

  • Role of Department Manager in Auditor Evaluation: Assign the sole authority for evaluating and appraising internal auditors to the Internal Audit Department Manager. This manager will be responsible for all assessments, independent of executive management influence. These evaluations must be conducted within the constraints of the existing appraising system quotas the company sets. This ensures the evaluation process aligns with the broader organizational policies and fairness in performance assessments across all departments.
  • Formalization of Evaluation Criteria: Establish clear, standardized criteria for evaluating IA performance. These criteria will be transparent, based on measurable objectives, and focused solely on the quality and effectiveness of audit work.
  • Prohibition of Executive Interference: Explicitly prohibit any executive, including the CEO, from altering or influencing the evaluations determined by the Internal Audit Department Manager.
  • Dispute Resolution Mechanism: Implement a formal process for addressing disagreements between IA and executive management regarding audit findings or evaluations. This process should involve a review by the Audit Committee.
  • Regular Training and Awareness Programs: Conduct regular training for auditors and executives on the importance of IA independence and ethical guidelines supporting unbiased auditing practices.

4. Stakeholder Engagement and Implementation

Engagement with key stakeholders such as IA staff, executive management, and the Board of Directors is crucial for successfully adopting these amendments. Gathering input and addressing concerns from these stakeholders will ensure that the proposed changes are well-received and effectively implemented.

5. Conclusion and Call to Action

We request the Audit Committee's approval to proceed with these proposed amendments to the Audit Charter. By enhancing auditor independence and objectivity provisions, [Company Name] will align more closely with best practices and standards advocated by the Institute of Internal Auditors and other regulatory bodies.
